Welcome

I am Fadee Kannah, a third year (junior) at San Diego State University who is majoring in computer engineering and minoring in computer science. I chose computer engineering as my major because of my passion towards technology and computers. I realized that my future will be based on this passion since I was twelve when I held a computer mouse for the first time.

I am currently the IT assistant for EOP (Educational Opportunity Programs and Ethnic Affairs) and College of Education at San Diego State University. I help facuilty and students who are experincing technological difficulties. I also Manage and maintain the department inventory of over $100k worth of hardware and software equipment. And I developed, and currently maintain, department website while meeting Federal ADA Compliances.

I am also the president and founder of Chaldean American Student Union (CASU). CASU is an organization that provides assistance and support to its members while introducing and promoting Chaldean and Middle Eastern cultures.

Also, I am currently the webmaster for the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) at San Diego State University.

In addition to that, I am a the president and one of the founders of HiTech EdVentures. HTEV is a new student organization and a business where students get contracted to work on projects supplied by the faculty of San Diego State University and/or industry.

Finally, I am a part of the Office of Educational Opportunity Programs and Ethnic Affairs, The Louis Stokes Alliance for Minority Participation and Mesa Engineering Progam.

Those are just a few of the things that I do on my free time. I always have at least a couple projects in progress at the same time. Such as developing software, designing websites, incorporating new things to old projects, and messing around with different software.
